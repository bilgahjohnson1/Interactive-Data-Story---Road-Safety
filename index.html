01 / The Scale

The Big Picture

From 2012 to 2025, Victoria recorded nearly 200,000 road crashes taking a significant toll on families, communities, and emergency services. Use the filters above to explore the data.

02 / When

Patterns in Time

  • Friday has the highest crash count (30,262).
  • Nearly 5.1% of all crashes happen on Friday 3–6 PM.
  • Crash volume dropped noticeably in 2020–2021 (COVID lockdowns), then rebounded sharply.
  • Wednesday–Friday consistently peaks, while Sunday records the lowest crashes.

    03 / Speed

    Speed & Severity

  • 110 km/h zones carry more than 5.0% fatality rate which the highest of any speed zone.
  • 100 km/h zones account for more fatalities despite lower crash volume than urban zones.
  • 50 km/h urban zones have the highest crash volume but only about 1% fatality rate.
  • The relationship shows: higher speed = lower crashes but far deadlier outcomes.

    04 / Who

    Demographics & Severity

  • Young adults represent the largest group involved.
  • Elderly 70+ have the highest fatality rate at 2.2%, despite lower involvement numbers.
  • Of 447,599 persons involved fatalities were 0.77%, but each was preventable.

    05 / Vehicles

    Vehicle Type Trends

  • Passenger cars dominate in volume , but motorcycles are 3.2× more likely to result in fatalities per crash.
  • Heavy vehicles have the highest fatality rate at 5.22%, reflecting the severe impact of crashes involving large, powerful vehicles.
  • Bicycle involvement has grown since 2019, reflecting increased cycling post-COVID
